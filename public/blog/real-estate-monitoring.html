Setting Up Real Estate Monitoring

Step 1: Find the Right URL

Most real estate sites let you save searches. The URL often contains your search criteria:

  • Go to Zillow, Redfin, or Realtor.com
  • Set your search filters (location, price, beds, etc.)
  • Copy the URL from your browser
üí° Pro Tip: The URL usually encodes your search. For example: zillow.com/homes/for_sale/12345_zip/0-500000_price/3-_beds/

Step 2: Use Keyword Alerts

Filter notifications to only what matters:

  • "price reduced" - Get alerts only on price drops
  • "new listing" - Know immediately when new homes are listed
  • "open house" - Never miss an open house
  • "pending" - Track when homes go under contract

Step 3: Set Check Frequency

In hot markets, check frequently:

  • Hot market: Every hour (Pro plan)
  • Normal market: Every 4-6 hours
  • Slow market: Daily

Limitations to Know

Some things to keep in mind:

  • Dynamic content: Some sites load listings via JavaScript. Monitor the initial page content.
  • Rate limits: Don't check too frequently or you may be blocked.
  • Mobile vs desktop: Some sites show different content. Use the desktop URL.
  • Login walls: PagePulse monitors public pages only.
