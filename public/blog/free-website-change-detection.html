What is Website Change Detection?

Website change detection automatically monitors webpages and alerts you when content changes. Instead of manually checking sites every day, these tools do the work for you and send notifications when something updates.

Common use cases include:

  • Price monitoring – Get alerts when product prices drop
  • Job hunting – Know immediately when new positions are posted
  • Competitor tracking – Monitor when competitors update their sites
  • Stock alerts – Get notified when items are back in stock
  • News monitoring – Track government pages or news sites

2. Visualping

Visualping is a popular option with a visual-first approach. The free tier includes 2 monitors with daily checks. However, webhook integrations require a paid plan.

3. Distill.io (Browser Extension)

Distill.io works as a browser extension, running checks from your local machine. This means you get more frequent checks (every 6 hours) but your computer must be running. The free cloud tier is very limited.

4. ChangeTower

ChangeTower focuses on archiving and comparing historical versions of pages. The free tier is limited to 2 monitors with weekly checks—too infrequent for most use cases.

When to Upgrade from Free

Free tiers work great for personal use and light monitoring. Consider upgrading when you need:

  • More monitors – Tracking more than 3 websites
  • Faster checks – Hourly instead of daily monitoring
  • Team access – Multiple users managing monitors
  • API volume – Heavy programmatic access

DIY Alternative: Build Your Own

For developers, you can build a simple change detector with a cron job and a script. However, this requires:

  • A server running 24/7
  • Handling webpage rendering (JavaScript sites)
  • Building notification integrations
  • Maintaining the code over time

For most people, a free tool like PagePulse is simpler and more reliable than maintaining your own solution.
