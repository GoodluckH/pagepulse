Competitor Website Monitoring: Track Changes Automatically

Updated February 2026 • 6 min read

Your competitors are making moves right now. Price changes, new features, messaging updates, job postings that signal expansion. By the time you notice manually, you're already behind.

Competitor monitoring automates this intelligence gathering, alerting you the moment something changes on a competitor's website.

What to Monitor on Competitor Websites

1. Pricing Pages

The most valuable intelligence. Know immediately when competitors:

  • Raise or lower prices
  • Add new pricing tiers
  • Change feature inclusions
  • Launch promotions or discounts

2. Product/Feature Pages

Track when competitors ship new features or deprecate old ones. Monitor:

  • Feature comparison tables
  • Product pages and descriptions
  • Integration listings
  • API documentation (for tech products)

3. Careers Pages

Job postings reveal strategic direction:

  • Hiring for AI/ML? They're investing in that area
  • Multiple sales roles? Aggressive expansion incoming
  • New market job postings? Geographic expansion

4. Blog & Content

Content strategy shifts indicate positioning changes:

  • New blog categories or topics
  • Case studies (reveals target customers)
  • Announcement pages

5. Homepage & Messaging

Positioning changes often start with homepage updates:

  • Hero text and value propositions
  • Social proof (customer logos, testimonials)
  • Call-to-action changes

How to Set Up Competitor Monitoring

Step 1: Identify Key Pages

For each competitor, list the most strategic pages:

  • Pricing: competitor.com/pricing
  • Features: competitor.com/features
  • Careers: competitor.com/careers
  • Blog: competitor.com/blog

Step 2: Create Monitors

Add each URL to PagePulse. For best results:

  • Use CSS selectors to focus on specific content (ignore ads, dates)
  • Set appropriate check frequency (daily for most, hourly for pricing)
  • Add descriptive names: "Acme Corp - Pricing Page"

Step 3: Configure Alerts

Route alerts to where your team will see them:

  • Slack channel for real-time team awareness
  • Email for individual tracking
  • Webhook to feed into your CRM or analytics
Pro tip: Create a dedicated Slack channel like #competitor-intel and route all competitor monitoring alerts there. Makes it easy for the whole team to stay informed.

Competitor Monitoring Best Practices

Focus on Actionable Intelligence

Don't monitor everything—focus on changes you can act on:

  • Price drops → Evaluate your pricing
  • New features → Assess product roadmap
  • Hiring surge → Prepare for increased competition

Track Multiple Competitors

Monitor at least 3-5 key competitors to spot industry trends vs. individual moves.

Historical Context Matters

Keep a log of changes over time. PagePulse stores check history so you can see patterns.

Combine with Other Intelligence

Website monitoring is one piece. Combine with:

  • Social media monitoring
  • Review site tracking
  • News alerts
  • SEC filings (for public companies)
